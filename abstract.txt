We estimate the three Herschel-Bulkley parameters (yield stress sigma_Y, power-law index n, consistency parameter eta) of complex fluids -- pastes, chunky sauces, granular slurries -- for which conventional rheometers cannot provide useful measurements, by matching dam-break video footage against physics-based simulation, following the protocol of [Hamamichi et al. 2023]. Each such inverse requires hundreds of in-loop material-point-method evaluations and therefore takes hours of GPU time per video; the identifiability mitigation proposed by prior work -- a multi-setup protocol guided by a Hessian analysis of the similarity relation in the loss landscape -- inherits this per-iterate simulation cost, putting routine per-material use out of reach.

We compress this inverse from hours to a median of twelve seconds per video by replacing the in-loop simulator with a hierarchical mixture of Gaussian-process experts trained on a corpus of MPM dam-break simulations and routed by a learned Bayesian gate. We address the same identifiability degeneracy through a complementary, surrogate-native path: we warm-start the optimizer from the routed expert's nearest training neighbor, anchor it with a soft prior on log-sigma_Y at that neighbor, and combine all available setups in a single joint objective rather than a sequential refinement.

We validate our method by comparing against the simulation-in-the-loop predecessor on real-video captures and against parallel-plate rheometer measurements on materials the rheometers can handle. The seconds-scale inversion enables three uses inaccessible to prior work: tracking parameter freshness for time-varying fluids across repeated captures, characterizing rheometer-infeasible materials as a routine task rather than a dedicated experiment, and a multi-observation joint protocol that approaches parallel-plate precision from phone videos alone.
