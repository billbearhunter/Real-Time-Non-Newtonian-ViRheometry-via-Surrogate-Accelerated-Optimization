\begin{abstract}
Non-Newtonian video rheometry~\cite{hamamichi2023nonnewtonian}
estimates Herschel--Bulkley parameters $(\sigma_Y, \eta, n)$ of complex
fluids by matching observed dam-break flows against physics-based
simulation, enabling the characterization of materials---pastes, chunky
sauces, granular fluids---that conventional rheometers cannot handle.
However, inverting the material point method in the loop requires hours
of GPU time per video, leaving this otherwise uniquely capable approach
confined to offline research use.

We propose a surrogate-accelerated framework that compresses this
inverse from hours to seconds per video. Our technical core couples two
components. First, a regime-aware hierarchical Mixture-of-GP surrogate
with 540 specialized sparse GP experts, routed by a two-level Bayesian
Gaussian mixture gate over container geometry and observation
signatures, and aggregated via the robust Bayesian Committee Machine.
Second, an identifiability-aware inverse in which CMA-ES is warm-started
from the routed expert's nearest training neighbor and anchored by a
soft log-$\sigma_Y$ prior at that neighbor---resolving the
yield-stress/consistency similarity degeneracy whose practical
mitigation had previously been computationally out of reach.

Evaluated against simulation-in-the-loop inversion on held-out synthetic
samples and real non-Newtonian fluids, our method matches baseline
accuracy at three orders of magnitude lower latency. This seconds-scale
inversion unlocks three capabilities inaccessible to prior work:
\emph{(i)} fresh parameter estimates for time-varying fluids,
\emph{(ii)} practical characterization of materials conventional
rheometers cannot measure, and \emph{(iii)} a multi-observation joint
protocol that approaches parallel-plate rheometer precision from phone
videos alone.
\end{abstract}
