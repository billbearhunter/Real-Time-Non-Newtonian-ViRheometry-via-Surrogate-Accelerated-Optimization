\begin{abstract}
Video-based rheometry estimates the material parameters of non-Newtonian
fluids by matching physical experiments with simulations, enabling the
measurement of materials for which conventional rheometers are less
effective.
However, existing methods require iterative physics simulations during
optimization, resulting in estimation times of several hours on high-end
GPUs and limiting their practical use.

We propose a surrogate-accelerated framework for non-Newtonian
viRheometry that replaces expensive simulation evaluations with a learned
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Gaussian process surrogate.
Our method consists of three stages:
(1) efficient training data collection,
(2) construction of a regime-aware surrogate in which a gating model
assigns specialized GP experts to distinct flow behaviors, and
(3) surrogate-driven CMA-ES optimization for fast parameter inference.

Our method achieves estimation accuracy comparable to a
simulation-based baseline while substantially reducing optimization time.
This acceleration moves inverse viRheometry toward real-time use and
enables capabilities that are difficult to achieve with prior methods,
including fresher parameter estimates for time-varying materials,
repeated estimation in short operational cycles, and improved practical
deployability.
\end{abstract}
